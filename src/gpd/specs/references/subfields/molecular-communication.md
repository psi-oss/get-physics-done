# Molecular Communication

Molecular Communication (MC) uses molecules as carriers of information rather than electromagnetic waves, serving as a foundational pillar of the Arkhe(n) stack. This subfield bridges biological coordination (e.g., *Paramecium* ciliary synchronization) and 6G network engineering in wave-constrained environments.

## Arkhe(n) Formal Mapping

In the Arkhe(n) framework, molecular signals are processed through the following stack:

- **Information Carriers**: Molecules are treated as **phase packets $\mathbb{C}$**—chemical quanta of information.
- **Propagation Medium**: Fluids, air, or biological tissues represent the **$\mathbb{R}^3$ space**.
- **Encoding Structure**: Concentration, timing, and molecular type define the **$\mathbb{Z}$ structure**.
- **Reception & Action**: Binding to receptors and subsequent biological response is the **projection $\mathbb{R}^4$**, where information transforms into action.
