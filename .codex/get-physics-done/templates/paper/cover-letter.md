# Cover Letter Template

Template for journal submission cover letters. Accompanies the manuscript.

---

## File Template

```markdown
---
journal: [Journal Name]
manuscript_title: "[Title]"
date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
corresponding_author: [Name, Email]
---

[Date]

Dear Editor,

We submit for your consideration our manuscript entitled "[Title]" for publication in [Journal].

### Key Result

[One paragraph: the main result and why it matters. Be specific — "We compute X and find Y" not "We study Z." Include at least one quantitative statement: a numerical value, a scaling relation, or a bound.]

### Novelty

[One paragraph: what is new compared to existing literature. Reference 2-3 specific prior works by author name and explain the advance beyond each. "Previous calculations by Smith et al. (2023) were limited to leading order; we extend to NNLO and find a 15% correction that resolves the tension with experimental data."]

### Significance

[One paragraph: why this matters for the field. What does this enable or resolve? Connect to broader questions: "This resolves the long-standing discrepancy between lattice and perturbative determinations of X" or "This enables precision tests of Y at the upcoming Z facility."]

### Suggested Referees

1. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] — Expert in [relevant topic]
2. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] — Expert in [relevant topic]
3. [Name], [Affiliation], [Email] — Expert in [relevant topic]

### Excluded Referees (optional)

[Name] — [Brief reason, e.g., "competing group with overlapping unpublished work"]

Sincerely,
[Authors]
```

## Guidelines

**Key Result paragraph:**
- Must contain a quantitative statement (number, scaling, bound)
- "We compute the two-loop beta function and find beta_2 = -0.47(3)" not "We study the beta function"
- Lead with the result, not the method

**Novelty paragraph:**
- Must reference specific papers, not vague "previous work"
- Explain what was done before and what advance this paper makes
- Use concrete comparisons: "extends from LO to NNLO", "first calculation including X effect"

**Suggested referees:**
- Select from different institutions and subfields
- Avoid close collaborators of the authors
- 3 referees minimum; provide valid institutional email addresses
- Choose people who will appreciate the work but can critically evaluate it

**Journal-specific guidance:**
- **PRL**: Emphasize broad significance and impact. Cover letter should argue why this is a Letter (broad interest, timely, significant advance) rather than a regular article
- **PRD/PRB/PRC**: Emphasize technical advance and completeness. Less pressure on broad impact, more on thoroughness
- **JHEP**: Focus on theoretical novelty and connections to formal developments
- **Nature Physics**: Must argue for impact beyond the immediate subfield

**Length:** Cover letter should be ≤ 1 page

**Checklist before sending:**
- [ ] Key result paragraph contains a quantitative statement
- [ ] Novelty paragraph references specific prior works (not just "previous studies")
- [ ] Significance paragraph connects to broader field questions
- [ ] Suggested referees are from different institutions
- [ ] No referees who are close collaborators of the authors
- [ ] Journal-appropriate framing (Letter vs regular article)
- [ ] Corresponding author contact information is correct
